Functionality

The main functionality of these plots is intended to be minimalistic and have a high data to ink ratio. I focused on maximizing the plot space by setting the margins low and increasing the dot size so the data stands out over any labels. While grid lines are useful in the scatterplot matrix, I have removed them from the plain bubble chart so as to lessen the amount of distraction in the visual.

Additionally, I have chosen a color scheme that does not emphasize one category over another. This categorical grouping is intended to merely separate regions of the US and not highlight any one region.

I have also chosen to group the categories of "Northeast" and "North Central" from the original data into one category, "North". This preserves the overall trend in the data (as seen in the scatterplot matrix) that states within these regions naturally group together in some areas. The reduction to only three categories increases simplicity in the visual and reduces clutter.

Brushing within the scatterplot matrix makes it especially easy for viewers to highlight the same data points across all plots. This is an important aspect which allows much data to be presented to the user without losing the ability to look at very specific details within the data.

In the large scatterplot, the x-axis range is limited from 67 to 74. Although this increases the lie factor, I felt it was unnecessary to view the full range starting from 0 since the variable "Life Expectancy" is never referrenced in a range that low. The visual becomes more informative when the range is viewed only for values that the data takes on. It was also relevant in this aspect to format the numbers with one decimal point. Because the range for average life expectancy across states is so small, it is more informative to mark half years as well as full years.
